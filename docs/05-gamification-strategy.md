# 05 · Gamification Strategy

> **Mandate:** a reward system that motivates *without* addictive mechanics.

For 3–6 year-olds, the goal is to encourage exploration, repetition of healthy
skills, and a sense of "I can do this" — **not** to maximize screen time or
engagement metrics. This document defines what we use, what we deliberately
refuse to use, and why.

---

## 1. Guiding principles

1. **Intrinsic over extrinsic.** The real reward is mastering a feeling skill
   and feeling proud. Tokens are gentle celebration, not the point.
2. **Participation, not performance.** Rewards come from *trying and engaging*,
   never from being "right," fast, or beating others. There is no failure
   state, so there's nothing to grind against.
3. **Calm celebration.** Warm, brief, low-arousal feedback. No slot-machine
   confetti storms, no escalating jackpots.
4. **No pressure loops.** No streaks that punish a missed day, no countdown
   timers, no "you'll lose it" loss-aversion, no daily login coercion.
5. **Finite & satisfying.** Collections are *completable*. A child can "finish"
   and feel done — we don't manufacture endless treadmills.
6. **The grown-up holds the dials.** Session limits, notifications, and any
   nudges live in Grown-Up Mode and default to gentle.

---

## 2. What we use ✅

### a) Emotion Card collection (the headline mechanic)
- 10 cards, one per emotion, collected by engaging with Emotion Explorer.
- **Educational by design:** each card's "back" teaches the feeling
  (definition, example, what helps). The collection *is* the curriculum.
- **Completable:** 10 and done. Completing the set unlocks a warm "You know all
  your feelings!" moment — not a new grind.

### b) Stickers (participation tokens)
- Earned for *finishing an activity* (any outcome), not for correctness.
- Themed to skills ("Brave Voice 💪", "Calm 🎈", "Kind 💜") so the reward
  reinforces the *behavior's name*.
- Displayed on a personal **Sticker Book shelf** — a keepsake, never a
  scoreboard. No quantity targets shown as quotas.

### c) Friends met (gentle progression)
- Visiting each world introduces a guide character (Mochi, Pip, Fen, Luna…).
- Light "keep exploring" curiosity (a ??? friend) — discovery, not FOMO.

### d) Warm acknowledgment
- Short, sincere praise tied to the *effort/skill*: "You used your brave
  voice!" "You took good care of your feelings." 
- Praise is **process-focused** ("Great thinking!") not trait-focused ("You're
  so smart!") — aligned with growth-mindset research.

### e) Real-world bridge (Kindness Missions)
- Empathy Lab gives offline, real-life prompts ("give someone a smile today").
- The best "reward" pulls the child *off the screen* into practicing the skill
  with real people. Parents can acknowledge completion in the dashboard.

---

## 3. What we deliberately refuse ❌

| Anti-pattern | Why it's banned here |
|---|---|
| **Streaks / "don't break the chain"** | Punishes normal life; creates anxiety & compulsion in kids and guilt in parents. |
| **Countdown timers / time-pressure** | Stress is antithetical to emotional regulation; harms kids who need more time. |
| **Variable/random reward schedules** ("loot boxes," surprise spins) | The core engine of compulsion. Never used. |
| **Leaderboards / social comparison** | Shame and competition undermine SEL for this age. |
| **Scores, stars-out-of-3, percentages, "wrong" buzzers** | Introduce failure & ranking we explicitly reject. |
| **Infinite/endless content treadmills** | We favor completable, satisfying arcs. |
| **Push notifications that nag** ("Come back! You'll lose…") | Loss-aversion manipulation. Reminders are opt-in, gentle, parent-controlled. |
| **Energy/lives/hearts that gate play** | Monetizable friction = pressure. None. |
| **In-app purchases or ads in Child Mode** | Never. (Business model lives in parent space — see roadmap.) |
| **Autoplay "up next" that chains sessions** | Each activity ends with a calm stop, not a pull into the next. |

---

## 4. Healthy session design

- **Natural stopping points.** Every activity ends with a clear, calm
  "you're done" beat and a return to Home — an easy place to put the device
  down.
- **Optional gentle session limit** (Grown-Up Mode): e.g. 15 min, after which
  Mochi says "Let's take a break and play outside!" and the app winds down
  warmly (no abrupt lockout, no countdown anxiety).
- **No "one more" mechanics.** We never bait continued play; we make stopping
  feel good.
- **Quality of engagement > quantity.** Success = a child who learned to name
  "frustrated" and tried a breath — even if they used the app for 4 minutes.

---

## 5. How rewards map to learning goals

| Reward | Reinforces |
|---|---|
| Emotion Cards | Naming & understanding the 10 emotions |
| Brave Voice sticker | Practicing assertive communication & boundaries |
| Calm sticker | Self-regulation as a repeatable habit |
| Kind sticker / Missions | Empathy & prosocial behavior in the real world |
| Choice sticker | Reflective decision-making |
| "Friends met" | Comfort, safety, and exploration of all skill areas |

---

## 6. Measuring success (without engagement-maxxing)

We measure **learning and wellbeing**, not stickiness. North-star and guardrail
metrics for the team:

- **North star:** breadth of emotional vocabulary engaged (distinct emotions a
  child has explored/check-in'd) and **repeat use of regulation tools** (did
  Calm Corner get used during a hard moment?).
- **Guardrails (we want these to stay LOW/healthy):** average session length
  (we are *happy* if it's short), no growth in late-night use, low reliance on
  reminders.
- **Parent-reported outcomes:** survey — "Has your child named a feeling or
  used a brave phrase in real life?" That real-world transfer is the true win.
- **Explicitly NOT optimized:** DAU-for-its-own-sake, session count, time-in-app
  as a goal.

This keeps the product honest: Feel Friends succeeds when a child needs it
*less* often because they've internalized the skills.
