# 08 · Content Library

The actual SEL content — emotions, story scenarios, brave phrases, calm
activities — written in child-directed language. This is the curriculum that
the worlds in [`01-information-architecture.md`](01-information-architecture.md)
deliver. Copy here is the **narrated** voice of the app.

---

## 1. The 10 core emotions

Each card has: name · friend face · color · kid-definition · example · "what
helps." Definitions are deliberately simple, body-based, and non-judgmental.

| Emotion | Color | Kid-definition (narrated) | Example | What helps |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **happy** | yellow | "Happy is a sunny feeling inside. You might smile or want to giggle." | "You got to play your favorite game." | "Share it! Tell someone what made you happy." |
| **sad** | soft blue | "Sad is a heavy, droopy feeling. Sometimes your eyes get teary." | "Your friend went home." | "It's okay to cry. A hug or a grown-up can help." |
| **angry** | warm red | "Angry is a hot, big feeling. Your body might feel tight." | "Someone broke your tower." | "Take a slow breath. Use your words, not your hands." |
| **frustrated** | orange | "Frustrated is when something is hard and you want to give up." | "Your zipper won't go up." | "Take a breath and ask, 'Can I have help?'" |
| **nervous** | yellow-green | "Nervous is a wobbly, fluttery tummy feeling before something new." | "It's your first day at a new place." | "Breathe slow. You can be brave even when you're nervous." |
| **scared** | violet | "Scared is when something feels too big or surprising." | "You heard a loud noise." | "Find a grown-up. You are safe. Let's breathe together." |
| **proud** | golden | "Proud is a tall, warm feeling when you did something hard." | "You tied your shoes by yourself!" | "Say it out loud: 'I did it!'" |
| **lonely** | slate-blue | "Lonely is when you wish someone was with you." | "No one is playing with you." | "You can ask, 'Can I play too?'" |
| **excited** | pink | "Excited is a bouncy, can't-wait feeling!" | "It's almost your birthday." | "Wiggle it out, then take a breath to feel calm-excited." |
| **confused** | warm grey | "Confused is when things don't make sense yet." | "You don't know what to do next." | "That's okay! Ask a question: 'Can you show me?'" |

> Note: "what helps" intentionally cross-links to Brave Voice phrases and the
> Calm Corner — the content is woven, not siloed.

## 2. Story Adventures — scenarios

Each scenario follows: **Scene → "What could you do?" → 2–3 choices → consequence
→ reflection → replay.** Choices include one assertive/helpful, one passive, and
one aggressive option (where age-appropriate); *all* play out without shaming.

### S1 · Toy sharing conflict  *(MVP)*
- **Scene:** You're playing with a truck. Another child wants it.
- **Choices:**
  1. 🗣️ "We can take turns. You can have it next." → friend smiles; you both play. *"That felt good!"*
  2. 😠 Hold it tight and say "No! Mine!" → friend looks sad; play feels yucky. *"Hmm, want to try another way?"*
  3. 😢 Give it up and feel bad → you feel lonely. → "You can share *and* still get a turn. Try again?"
- **Reflection:** "How did your friend feel?" → feeling faces.

### S2 · Being pushed  *(MVP)*
- **Scene:** In line, someone pushes you.
- **Choices:**
  1. 🗣️ "Please stop. I don't like that." then tell a grown-up if it continues. → *brave!* 
  2. 😠 Push back → now both are upset; a grown-up steps in. → "Pushing back can hurt. Try your brave words?"
  3. 🤐 Say nothing and feel upset → "Your feelings matter. You can say 'Please stop.'"
- **Bridge:** "Practice 'Please stop.' in Brave Voice?"

### S3 · Being excluded from play  *(MVP)*
- **Scene:** Two friends are playing and say "You can't play."
- **Choices:**
  1. 🗣️ "Can I play too?" → sometimes yes! Sometimes find another friend. Both are okay.
  2. 😢 Walk away feeling lonely → "That's a sad feeling. You're allowed to ask to join."
  3. 😠 Knock down their game → "That made it worse. Let's try asking instead."
- **Reflection:** names "lonely"; bridges to "Can I play too?"

### S4 · Asking for help  *(MVP)*
- **Scene:** You can't reach your cup / open the snack.
- **Choices:**
  1. 🗣️ "Can I have help, please?" → a grown-up helps; you feel relieved & proud.
  2. 😣 Keep struggling and get frustrated → "Asking for help is brave and smart."
  3. 😢 Give up → "It's okay to need help. Everyone does!"

### S5 · Being teased  *(v1.1)*
- **Scene:** Someone calls your drawing "ugly."
- **Choices:** "I don't like that." + walk to a grown-up · believe it & feel sad
  (→ reframed: "Their words don't make it true") · tease back (→ "That keeps the
  hurt going").

### S6 · Taking turns  *(v1.1)*
- **Scene:** Everyone wants the swing.
- **Choices:** suggest counting turns · grab it · wait sadly → guided to a fair,
  happy turn-taking outcome.

## 3. Brave Voice — phrases

Core 4 (MVP), each with: model audio, when-to-use, and an echo/record practice.

| Phrase | When to use (narrated) |
|---|---|
| **"Please stop."** | "When someone is doing something you don't like to your body or things." |
| **"I don't like that."** | "When something feels unkind or uncomfortable. Your feelings are okay to share." |
| **"Can I have help?"** | "When something is too hard, or you feel unsafe. Asking is brave!" |
| **"Can I play too?"** | "When you want to join in and you feel left out." |

**Situational add-ons (v1.1+):** "It's my turn now." · "That hurt my feelings." ·
"No, thank you." · "I need a break."

Coaching: model with a **confident, kind, medium volume** voice — not yelling,
not whispering. The app praises *using the voice*, never the recording's
"quality."

## 4. Calm Corner — activities

| Activity | How it works | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Balloon Breathing** *(MVP)* | Balloon inflates on a 4s in-breath, deflates on a 6s out-breath, 3–5 cycles, soft tone + narration. | Extended exhale calms the nervous system; visual pacing teaches breath control. |
| **Glitter Jar** *(MVP)* | Shake/tap to swirl glitter, then watch it slowly settle. | A concrete metaphor: "busy mind settles like the glitter." |
| **Counting Calm (5-4-3-2-1)** *(v1.1)* | Find 5 things you see, 4 you hear, etc. (icon-guided). | Grounding/sensory regulation for big feelings. |
| **Quiet Listen** *(v1.1)* | A 30–60s soft soundscape with a gentle "just listen" prompt. | Introduces simple mindfulness/stillness. |

All calm activities: no timer pressure, no score, calm palette/audio, exit any
time, no reward fanfare (the calm is the reward).

## 5. Empathy Lab — content

- **"How does this friend feel?"** *(MVP)* — a character in a situation (dropped
  ice cream, won a game, lost a toy, got a hug); child taps the matching
  feeling face; then a kindness response.
- **Perspective swap** *(v1.2)* — the same scene shown from two children's
  points of view ("you felt happy sharing — your friend felt happy too").
- **Kindness Missions** *(MVP, offline real-world):* "Give someone a smile." ·
  "Help put toys away." · "Say something kind to a friend." · "Give a gentle
  hug (if they want one)." Parents can mark these done in the dashboard.

## 6. Good Choice Challenge — scenarios *(3 in MVP)*

| Scenario | Choices | Positive outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Clean-up time but you want to keep playing | help clean / refuse | "Clean-up is faster together — then more play time!" |
| Friend is sad | comfort them / ignore | "Your kindness helped your friend feel better." |
| You made a mistake (spilled) | tell a grown-up / hide it | "Telling the truth is brave. Mistakes are okay." |

More scenarios (waiting your turn, gentle hands, sharing snacks) in v1.2.

## 7. AI Story Generator — content guardrails *(v1.1)*

Generated stories MUST:
- Use the child's name and the parent-described situation.
- Stay **gentle, hopeful, and resolution-positive** (the child copes/learns).
- Center one of the 10 core emotions and a coping skill or brave phrase.
- Be **short** (~6–10 simple sentences), present a feeling → a helpful
  action → a warm resolution.

Generated stories MUST NOT:
- Include anything frightening, violent, unsafe, medical-specific, or
  values-loaded beyond the parent's input.
- Name real third parties, contain PII beyond the child's first name, or
  reference the outside world unsafely.
- Surface to a child without **parent review and explicit save**.

Template skeleton the model fills:
> "{Name} felt {emotion} because {situation}. {Name}'s body felt {body cue}.
> Then {Name} remembered {coping skill / brave phrase}. {Name} tried it…
> and {warm, realistic resolution}. {Name} felt proud for being brave."

See [`09-safety-privacy-accessibility.md`](09-safety-privacy-accessibility.md)
for the full safety pipeline.
